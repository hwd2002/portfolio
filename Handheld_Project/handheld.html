Handleld Raspberry Pi Project

My summer project was to use a Raspberry Pi I was given and incorporate it into something. I decided to make a handheld "cyberdeck" (what does that even mean?) and use some 3d modeling software I had learned about in College, specifically CATIA.

I made a requirement for the project to not alter the Pi itself so it could still be taken out and repurposed at a moment's notice. Additionally I chose to reuse as many parts lying around that I had. This resulted in the PSP thumbstick and the analog stick not matching.

The keyboard is removable and uses Bluetooth.

Yes, you can use a pencil as a stand.

The project had to be downgraded several times as power issues kept occuring. Despite the large 6600mah battery, the charging circuit couldn't handle enough to power the Pi 5. A Pi 3 was substituted in, limiting the capability of the device significantly. Additionally there were issues with fitting all the components inside due to the angled case. Despite these drawbacks I consider the project a success.

An Adafruit Feather functions as a daughter board. It is also removable and is not soldered to any other components.

The entire assembly snaps together, with the keyboard and front plate being friction fit.

The CATIA assembly was used to measure out components internally. This caused issues when the cables were not accounted for.

The overall design was inspired by a CRT TV, oddly enough.
